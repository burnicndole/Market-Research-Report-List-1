In agriculture, the use of these agents in fertilizers enhances the efficiency of nutrient absorption, particularly in iron-deficient soils.</p><p>Moreover, the trend toward environmentally friendly and biodegradable chelating agents is gaining traction, with a focus on sustainable practices. <p><p>The iron chelating agent market comprises three main types: Deferasirox, Deferoxamine, and Deferiprone. Deferasirox is an oral medication primarily used for chronic iron overload, offering convenience and improved patient compliance. Deferoxamine, usually administered through injections, is effective for acute iron toxicity and chronic conditions but has a more complex administration schedule. Deferiprone is an oral chelator often used in patients with thalassemia, known for its efficient iron removal but may require monitoring for side effects. Each agent is tailored for specific clinical needs.</p></p>
